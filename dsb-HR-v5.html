D. Leavers


If managers do not indicate the reason for an employee’s departure on the form submitted to HR, the reason is recorded as ‘Unknown’. Leavers data is reported in the month that it was recorded if this was later than the actual month of the change.

  • Voluntary leavers include resignations, career breaks, retirements and voluntary redundancies.
  • Involuntary leavers include dismissals, the cessation of temporary contracts, ill health retirements, mutual terminations, compulsory redundancies and transfers out of the organisation (i.e. TUPE transfers).

E. Absence - % days lost


The % days lost is the average number of days absence per full time equivalent (FTE) divided by the number of working days in the month.
The mean absence rate reported by the Local Government Association Workforce Survey 2012/13 is 3.8% for upper tier authorities.

R. Take up of staff benefits


The Council currently offers a small number of ad hoc benefits to staff such as staff discounts (Devon Discounts) and two ‘salary sacrifice’ schemes (Cycle to Work and Childcare Vouchers). The salary sacrifice schemes enable the Council and employees to make savings on ‘Pay as you Earn’ tax and National Insurance contributions.

S. Staff training


The Council is committed to the ongoing development of its staff and offers a range of development opportunities. The graph above shows the take-up of current opportunities, which commenced in November 2013, that are available to support employees through the service transition/redundancy programmes, and also support redeployment. As 92% of our staff live in Devon, this programme also encourages engagement in local communities, as volunteers or as future partners working in mutual, social enterprises or with private sector providers.
