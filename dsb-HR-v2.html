Human Resources

The HR data dashboard.
NB This sample is a prototype in early development stage; all feedback gratefully received.

A. Headcount


Number of assignments (job count - actual number of people employed) and full time equivalents (FTEs - which translates these numbers into full time hours worked overall), excluding: Devon Shared Audit Partnership staff, centrally employed teachers in Personalised Learning services and Library & Information Services staff on variable hours contracts.
Headcount numbers continue to reduce as a result of Vacancy Management (between 2009-2012) and the current Redundancy and Turnover Management arrangements.

B. Turnover


Shows the numbers of those staff joining the Council balanced against those leaving.
Voluntary turnover includes resignations, career breaks, retirements and voluntary redundancies.
Involuntary turnover includes dismissals, the cessation of temporary contracts, ill health retirements, mutual terminations, compulsory redundancies and transfers out of the organisation (i.e. TUPE transfers).
If managers do not indicate the reason for an employee’s departure on the form submitted to HR, the reason is recorded as ‘Unknown’.
Starters and leavers data is reported in the month that it was recorded if this was later than the actual month of the change.
Turnover has increased recently as a result of the various service change programmes.

C. Absence - % days lost


The % days lost is the average number of days absence per FTE divided by the number of working days in the month.
The mean absence rate reported by the Local Government Association Workforce Survey 2012/13 is 3.8% for upper tier authorities.

D. Absence - number of days lost


The average days lost is the total number of days absence in the month, divided by the FTE.

D. Redundancy


Shows the numbers of voluntary and compulsory redundancies, with a peak in October due to Adult Social Care Residential Home closures and Childrens Social Work Services and Child Protection: Youth Services Restructure. All staff at risk of redundancy are subject to redeployment procedures.

E. Redundancy costs


As per the redundancies the high costs represent a large number of redundancies (121) due to the Adult Social Care Residential Home closures and Childrens Social Work Services and Child Protection.

F. Redeployment


The number in brackets shows the actual number of employees redeployed in that month. These are low relative to the numbers active on the register, as the availability of potential redeployment opportunities reduces as the organisation contracts.
The number on the deployment register at December 2014 has been left blank while data is validated; the correct updated figure will be available shortly.

G. Redeployment savings


Each redeployment leads to savings in redundancy pay, with £226,000 saved through successful redeployment since the introduction of Redundancy and Turnover Management. Steps have also been taken to enable increased redeployment to other sectors, such as through working with the Job Centre.

H. Overtime and temporary staff hours


"Temp hours" shows the number of hours worked each month by the casuals and agency staff managed centrally by Temp Solutions - the Temp Bank.
The following areas use casuals which are not currently included as they are not through Temp Solutions: School Escorts, Traffic Census, Intervention Service, Devon Action and Frontiers, Youth Service, ACL, Registration Service.
"Overtime hours" shows the number of contractual overtime hours worked by permanent staff.

I. Overtime and temporary staff costs


This is the cost of casuals and agency staff managed centrally by Temp Solutions, and of contractual overtime worked by permanent staff.

J. Take up of staff benefits


The Council currently offers a small number of ad hoc benefits to staff such as staff discounts (Devon Discounts) and two ‘salary sacrifice’ schemes (Cycle to Work and Childcare Vouchers). The salary sacrifice schemes enable the Council and employees to make savings on ‘Pay as you Earn’ tax and National Insurance contributions.

K. Staff training


The Council is committed to the ongoing development of its staff and offers a range of development opportunities. The graph above shows the take-up of current opportunities, which commenced in November 2013, that are available to support employees through the service transition/redundancy programmes, and also support redeployment. As 92% of our staff live in Devon, this programme also encourages engagement in local communities, as volunteers or as future partners working in mutual, social enterprises or with private sector providers.

L. Pay scales


The Council employs staff on national conditions of service. Further information on the Council’s pay policy can be found at:
