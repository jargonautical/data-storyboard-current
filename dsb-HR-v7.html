Human Resources

The data story behind Human Resources.
NB This sample is a prototype in early development stage; all feedback gratefully received.

Headcount


These numbers exclude Devon Shared Audit Partnership staff, centrally employed teachers in Personalised Learning services and Library & Information Services staff on variable hours contracts.
Headcount numbers continue to reduce as a result of Vacancy Management (between 2009-2012) and the current Redundancy and Turnover Management arrangements.

  • Headcount: number of assignments (this is more than the actual number of people employed by the council, as many staff have more than one assignment and are therefore counted more than once)
  • Full time equivalents (FTEs): headcount numbers translated into full time hours worked overall)


Get the datasets: headcount.csv FTE.csv

Turnover


Turnover is calculated by dividing the number of assignments counted as voluntary leavers as a percentage of the mean number of Oracle assignments in the given period.
Turnover has increased recently as a result of the various service change programmes.

Starters


Starters data is taken from the monthly generic starters lists, and shows new employees who are paid on the payroll in that month. The data is reported in the month that it was recorded if this was later than the actual month of starting.

Get the dataset: starters.csv

Leavers


If managers do not indicate the reason for an employee’s departure on the form submitted to HR, the reason is recorded as ‘Unknown’. Leavers data is reported in the month that it was recorded if this was later than the actual month of the change.

  • Voluntary leavers include resignations, career breaks, retirements and voluntary redundancies.
  • Involuntary leavers include dismissals, the cessation of temporary contracts, ill health retirements, mutual terminations, compulsory redundancies and transfers out of the organisation (i.e. TUPE transfers).


Get the dataset: leavers.csv

Overtime and temporary staff hours


"Temporary staff hours" shows the number of hours worked each month by the casuals and agency staff managed centrally by Temp Solutions - the Temp Bank.
The following areas use casuals which are not currently included as they are not through Temp Solutions: School Escorts, Traffic Census, Intervention Service, Devon Action and Frontiers, Youth Service, ACL, Registration Service.
"Overtime hours" shows the number of non-contractual overtime hours worked by employees.

Overtime and temporary staff costs


This is the cost of casuals and agency staff managed centrally by Temp Solutions, and of non-contractual overtime worked by employees.

Get the datasets: temps.csv overtime.csv

Absence - % days lost


The % days lost is the average number of days absence per full time equivalent (FTE) divided by the number of working days in the month.
The mean absence rate reported by the Local Government Association Workforce Survey 2012/13 is 3.8% for upper tier authorities.

Absence - num. days lost


The average days lost is the total number of days absence in the month, divided by the full time equivalent (FTE).

Get the dataset: absence.csv

Top 5 reasons for absence


The number of days lost for the top five reasons for sickness absence (taken over a six-month comparison period, so there are more than five reasons listed in this chart). Reporting for absence for psychological illnesses is now being reported in greater detail, so from November 2014 the figure shown is the sum of the four categories that are included in the new reporting - Stress, Anxiety, Depression and 'Other'.

Get the dataset: absence reasons.csv or view the heatmap table for all recorded absence reasons: absence reasons (table)

Redundancy


Shows the numbers of voluntary and compulsory redundancies, with a peak in October due to Adult Social Care Residential Home closures and Childrens Social Work Services and Child Protection: Youth Services restructure. All staff at risk of redundancy are subject to redeployment procedures.

Redundancy costs


As per the redundancies the high costs represent a large number of redundancies (121) due to the Adult Social Care Residential Home closures and Childrens Social Work Services and Child Protection: Youth Services restructure.

Get the dataset: redundancy.csv

Redeployment


The number in brackets shows the actual number of employees redeployed in that month. These are low relative to the numbers active on the register, as the availability of potential redeployment opportunities reduces as the organisation contracts.
The number on the redeployment register at December 2014 is unavailable due to problems with data collection in that month.

Redeployment savings


Each redeployment can lead to savings in redundancy pay. Steps have also been taken to enable increased redeployment to other sectors, such as through working with the Job Centre.
